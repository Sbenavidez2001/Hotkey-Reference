The layers experienced engineers think in
each layer's decisions lock in the layers below — get the order right and the downstream work feels obvious
roles → auth → authz → schema → infra
01
Users & Jobs-to-be-Done
Who is this for, and what outcome do they need? Outcomes, not features.
02
Actors & Roles
How many distinct user types? Usually two to four. Seven means you're confusing roles with permissions.
03
Domain Entities
The nouns — Account, User, Property, Charge. Names and relationships only; no schemas yet.
04
Identity (AuthN)
How users prove who they are. SSO, magic links, password.
05
Authorization (AuthZ)
Which roles can do what to which entities. The single hardest layer to retrofit.
06
Data Model
Now the tables, columns, indexes. By this point most decisions have been forced by the layers above.
07
Surfaces & Boundaries
APIs, UI shells, what's public versus internal.
08
State, Flows & Side Effects
How data moves — what's transactional versus eventually consistent.
09
Infrastructure & Deployment
Where the code runs. Most teams start here — the most expensive mistake in the entire list.
10
Observability
How you'll know it's healthy.
